One of the biggest lessons I’ve learned working with AI over the past two years is that it does not need to be perfect.

It needs to be useful in the right ways:

- challenge my thinking and sharpen my reasoning
- explain and dissect ideas so I understand them more deeply
- give me a decent starting point so I can try, fail, and learn fast

I do not need AI to do the thinking for me.

I need it to raise the quality of my thinking.
